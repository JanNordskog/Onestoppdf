## Market gaps

- No polished, modern self-hostable all-in-one PDF suite exists among incumbents: all five are cloud-first, PDF24's self-hosted server is enterprise-only with unpublished pricing, and the demand is proven — Stirling PDF and offline Ghostscript/PDFtk are recommended in 'nearly every Reddit thread' and 'truly local processing' is the #1 recurring user wish.
- Privacy objection is structural, not vendor-specific: users with tax, medical (HIPAA/PHI), and legal (privilege-waiver) documents categorically refuse cloud upload even to ISO 27001/GDPR-certified vendors, because 'files deleted after 1 hour' is unverifiable. No amount of certification closes this gap — only an architecture where files never leave the user's machine/server does.
- PDF tooling and e-signing are never unified AND unmetered: PDF suites gate signing hard (Smallpdf ~5 docs/month free, Adobe 2 sends/30 days, iLovePDF spins it into a separate credit-metered product), while e-sign vendors offer no PDF tooling and meter aggressively (3 sends/month free is the industry standard; DocuSign meters even paid plans at ~100 envelopes/user/year — its #1 complaint).
- Every freemium competitor converts via artificial scarcity — 2 tasks/day (Smallpdf), 3/hour (Sejda), 1 per 30 days (Adobe), unpublished daily caps (iLovePDF) — and this is exactly what breaks real multi-step workflows (merge→compress→number hits the wall). A whole 'Sejda alternative' cottage industry exists because of one rate limit. Nobody competes on 'no meters.'
- Trust/billing gap: documented Trustpilot patterns of dark-pattern trials (Smallpdf charging ~$108 silently at trial end, no reminder or cancellation-confirmation emails), refused refunds (iLovePDF ~7-day window), and HN-level resentment of subscriptions for occasional use. Honest billing is an unclaimed differentiator.
- Filled-form data extraction (pulling key-value pairs from medical/legal/intake forms into CSV/JSON) is explicitly called out on HN as something 'no current tool does well' — none of the six competitor columns offers it, yet it is straightforward with AcroForm parsing.
- Free batch processing: only PDF24 offers it, wrapped in a UI reviewers call dated and cluttered; everyone else paywalls batch (Smallpdf Pro, iLovePDF Premium, Sejda single-file-per-task free).
- No live preview before destructive operations — iLovePDF's watermark tool applies blind ('placement is a guess'), and users explicitly wish for previews before watermark/compress operations.
- Cross-platform local processing is unserved: PDF24's free desktop app is Windows-only, Sejda's desktop is capped at 3 tasks/day, iLovePDF and Smallpdf paywall their desktop apps entirely, and HN users explicitly ask for a lightweight app rather than a Docker container.
- PDF-to-Word fidelity disappointment is universal (broken tables, shifted layouts across ALL tools) — the gap is not magic conversion but honest expectation-setting plus structured-output alternatives (text/Markdown/CSV), since even Adobe fails at layout preservation.

## Our differentiators

- Privacy by architecture, not policy: self-hosted (single Docker image / runnable JAR + Postgres), so files never leave the user's infrastructure. This converts the #1 recurring wish and the 'upload-and-pray' distrust of deletion claims into a structural guarantee — one that cloud incumbents cannot match without cannibalizing their own SaaS model. It is also the only answer HIPAA/legal-privilege users will accept.
- Zero artificial meters: no daily/hourly task caps, no file-size tiers, no watermarks, no ads, no account required for core use. Every documented conversion lever competitors use (Smallpdf 2/day, Sejda 3/hour, Adobe 1/30-days, iLovePDF 15 MB conversion cap) becomes a comparison-marketing bullet.
- Unlimited e-sign built into the same product: drag-drop fields, email-link signing with no recipient account, sequential multi-signer, audit-trail certificate, tamper-evidence — the market-scan's exact minimum viable set — with no envelope metering ever. This attacks DocuSign's #1 complaint (metered paid plans) and the PDF suites' 5-docs/2-sends free caps simultaneously; no competitor unifies unmetered signing with a full PDF toolkit.
- Filled-form data extraction to CSV/JSON as a headline tool — an explicitly named, completely unserved gap (all six competitor columns: no) that PDFBox's AcroForm API makes cheap to build; a wedge into legal/medical/ops teams who are also the self-host buyers.
- Live preview on every destructive operation (watermark placement, compression quality slider with before/after, crop) — directly fixes the documented iLovePDF no-preview complaint and a stated user wish.
- Batch processing and tool chaining free: because monetization is not per-task, multi-file queues and merge→compress→number pipelines are natural free features, weaponizing Sejda's 3/hour wall and iLovePDF's Premium-gated Workflows against them.
- Honest billing as a marketed feature: a published limits table (vs. iLovePDF/Smallpdf's opaque caps), email reminder before any trial converts, one-click cancellation with confirmation email, 30-day refunds — each line a direct counter to a documented Trustpilot complaint, turning competitors' trust deficit into acquisition copy.
- Flat pricing instead of per-seat/per-envelope metering, and cross-platform by default (browser UI on a JVM server runs on macOS/Linux where PDF24's free desktop app doesn't exist).
- Transparent conversion fidelity: publish a fidelity test-suite scoreboard and label extraction-grade PDF-to-Word honestly instead of overpromising — converting the category's universal disappointment into brand trust.

## MVP build priority

1. Core page toolkit — merge, split, rotate, delete/extract pages, and a visual drag-and-drop page organizer with thumbnails (pure PDFBox rendering). These are the highest-traffic tools on every competitor and carry zero conversion-fidelity risk; ship them with per-tool landing pages to copy the proven SEO pattern.
2. Compress PDF with a quality slider and side-by-side before/after preview (PDFBox image downsampling + re-encoding, user-controlled DPI/JPEG quality) — directly answers the 'blurry output, Ghostscript is better' complaints by giving users the control online tools hide.
3. Images-to-PDF and PDF-to-images (JPG/PNG both directions, DPI control, multi-page handling) via PDFBox rendering.
4. Watermark, page numbers, and headers/footers with live rendered preview before applying — fixes the documented iLovePDF blind-watermark complaint on day one.
5. Security basics: password protect/unlock (AES-256 via PDFBox), flatten, and view/edit/strip document metadata.
6. Form filling plus filled-form data extraction — fill AcroForm fields in-browser, and export key-value pairs from filled forms to CSV/JSON (PDFBox AcroForm API). Cheap to build, completely unserved, and the wedge feature for legal/medical self-host buyers.
7. E-sign core (electronic signatures): upload/draw/type signature with reusable saved signatures (answering the 'stop making me redraw' wish), drag-drop signature/initials/date/text/checkbox fields, email-link signing with no recipient account, sequential multi-signer ordering, automatic reminders and expiry, sent/viewed/signed status tracking, and an appended audit-trail certificate page (signer, timestamp, IP) with SHA-256 hash for tamper-evidence — the market scan's exact minimum viable feature set, all achievable with PDFBox drawing + app logic. Positioned as ESIGN/UETA-style electronic signing; cryptographic seals come in v2.
8. Batch mode on every tool plus a server-side job queue (multi-file upload, zip download) — free batch is a gap in every competitor's free tier except PDF24's.
9. PDF-to-Word/text/Markdown at honest extraction grade: PDFBox text+image extraction assembled into .docx via Apache POI (XWPF), plus plain text and Markdown outputs, with an explicit on-screen fidelity notice ('extraction-grade: text and images preserved, complex layouts simplified'). No cloud APIs, no false layout promises — differentiate on honesty since no competitor achieves fidelity anyway.
10. Self-host foundation: single Docker image / runnable JAR, Postgres-backed users and teams, configurable file-retention policy (including delete-immediately-after-download), job history, and an admin panel — the operational skeleton that makes 'your files never leave your server' a checkable claim.

## V2 roadmap

- Office-to-PDF (Word/Excel/PPT) via an optional headless LibreOffice sidecar container — still fully self-hosted and open-source, no cloud conversion APIs; kept out of MVP because pure-Java rendering of Office formats is not credible.
- OCR via Tesseract (tess4j) with unlimited pages — attacks the Premium/Pro OCR gates at iLovePDF, Smallpdf, Sejda (10-page free cap), and Adobe.
- True redaction: content-stream removal plus metadata scrub (not visual cover-up), marketed as verifiable — the 'true redaction outside expensive Acrobat' wish; built carefully post-MVP because a leaky redaction is a liability.
- Workflows: chain tools into saved pipelines (merge → compress → number → sign) and watch folders — free, in direct contrast to iLovePDF's Premium-gated Workflows and Sejda's rate-limit-broken chains.
- Cryptographic digital signatures via Bouncy Castle (PAdES, timestamps, long-term validation) — upgrades e-sign to parity with Smallpdf's Pro 'digital seal' and strengthens eIDAS positioning.
- E-sign growth pack: reusable templates (the universal e-sign upsell lever), public template/signing links, bulk send, and custom branding on signature requests.
- REST API + webhooks including embedded signing — self-hosted, no credit metering, contrasting with iLoveAPI credits and per-envelope API pricing.
- Compare PDFs, repair PDF, PDF/A conversion, and Bates numbering — the long-tail professional tools PDF24 and Sejda use to win power users.
- Compliance tier plumbing: SSO (OIDC/SAML), roles/permissions, org-wide audit logs — what the on-prem legal/medical buyer needs and what incumbents reserve for opaque enterprise quotes.
- Cloud-storage connectors (Google Drive, Dropbox, S3, WebDAV) — a documented PDF24 weakness and frequent user request.
- Column-aware PDF-to-Markdown/text conversion for multi-column and magazine layouts — an explicit HN wish no incumbent serves.
- Desktop packaging via jpackage (Windows/macOS/Linux) so non-technical users get a lightweight local app without touching Docker — the explicit HN critique of Stirling PDF's container-only distribution.

## Pricing recommendation

Open-core with flat, honest pricing — monetize organizational features, never task counts. (1) Community edition, free and open-source, self-hosted: every core tool including e-sign, no task caps, no file-size tiers, no watermarks, no ads — this occupies the proven 'Stirling PDF but polished, with signing' position and is the only credible answer to the privacy objection. (2) Pro server license, flat per-server rather than per-seat: ~$129/year per server, with a one-time ~$299 perpetual-license option (1 year of updates) to answer the explicit and repeated one-time-purchase wish (HN resentment of Adobe's ~$20/mo and hidden Foxit/Nitro perpetual options). Pro unlocks SSO, admin console and audit logs, e-sign branding/templates/bulk send, and the API — with 'unlimited signature envelopes, forever' as the marketing spear against DocuSign's metered paid plans (~100/user/year) and Smallpdf/Adobe's 5-docs/2-sends free caps. (3) Optional managed-cloud hosting at ~$6/user/month billed annually for people who won't self-host — undercutting Smallpdf ($9–15/mo), matching iLovePDF (€5/mo), and far below Adobe ($14.99–24.99/mo). (4) Ship a billing pledge as a product feature, each line countering a documented Trustpilot complaint: published limits table (vs. iLovePDF's unpublished daily cap and Smallpdf's vague limits), email reminder 3 days before any trial converts, one-click cancellation with a confirmation email, and 30-day no-questions refunds.

## Appendix B — user pain points

### Complaints
- iLovePDF: essential features like batch processing and larger file sizes are paywalled; free tier file-size limits make anything beyond a quick single task frustrating (G2/Capterra reviews)
- iLovePDF: Trustpilot pattern of unexpected subscription charges, refusal to refund annual plans (refund window only ~7 days), and unresponsive support when disputing charges
- Smallpdf: '7-day free trial' dark pattern — users report being charged a full ~$108 yearly subscription immediately or silently at trial end, with no reminder or cancellation-confirmation emails, and failed cancellation flows (Trustpilot)
- Smallpdf: refunds refused even when cancelled same-day as an auto-renewal; support just cites Terms and Conditions (Trustpilot)
- Smallpdf: free tier limited to ~2 documents/tasks per day, which interrupts any real multi-document workflow
- Sejda: 3-tasks-per-hour free limit breaks chained workflows (compress, then merge, then add page numbers = limit hit), plus 200-page / 50 MB caps on free tier
- PDF24: UI feels busy/outdated with many small tools crammed together; lacks deep layout editing and advanced form tools; desktop app is Windows-only (no macOS)
- iLovePDF mobile app: frequent crashes and loss of Favorites/Recent PDFs; ads and pop-ups in the free version interrupt workflow (App Store / justuseapp reviews)
- PDF-to-Word fidelity (all tools): recurring Reddit complaints about tables breaking apart, misaligned text and images, changed fonts and spacing, and lost page layout — no tool preserves formatting reliably
- PDF compression (Smallpdf/iLovePDF/Sejda): output images re-encoded to visibly blurry quality; Reddit threads routinely recommend a Ghostscript command as better than any online compressor
- Smallpdf OCR: very slow on large/image-heavy files, and scanned-PDF-to-Word requires Pro to get editable text — free users get an uneditable image dump
- iLovePDF watermark tool: no preview before applying, so placement/formatting is a guess
- Pricing model resentment (HN): Adobe ~$20/mo too expensive for occasional use; Foxit/Nitro hide one-time-purchase options; Smallpdf-style subscriptions cost more per year than Microsoft Office for simple operations

### Wishes
- Truly local processing — tools that run entirely in the browser (WASM/JavaScript) or on-device so files never leave the machine; this is the #1 recurring wish and the pitch of every fast-growing alternative (Stirling PDF, OneClickPDF, EasyPDF, etc.)
- No sign-up, no hourly/daily task limits, no watermarks — 'just let me do the task'
- One-time purchase or genuinely free instead of subscriptions; fair pricing for people who touch a PDF twice a month
- A lightweight cross-platform desktop app rather than a Docker container or web service (HN commenters on Stirling PDF)
- A static JavaScript-only PDF toolsite requiring zero server infrastructure (explicit HN request)
- High-fidelity PDF-to-Word conversion that actually preserves tables, columns, fonts, and layout
- Reliable form-data extraction — pulling key-value pairs from filled medical/legal forms (no current tool does this well, per HN)
- True redaction that removes data rather than visually hiding it, available outside expensive Acrobat
- Easy signature handling — uploading a pre-made signature image instead of hand-drawing every time
- Column-aware conversion of multi-column/magazine PDFs into readable text/Markdown
- PDF accessibility remediation tooling
- Batch processing on the free tier (currently paywalled on iLovePDF)
- Live preview before applying operations like watermarks
- Honest trial/billing UX: reminder before trial converts, cancellation confirmation emails, and no-hassle refunds
- Better/faster OCR for scanned documents without a Pro upgrade to unlock editable output

### Privacy concerns
- Fear of uploading sensitive documents (tax returns, bank statements, contracts, medical records with PHI, attorney-client legal files) to third-party servers; community consensus is that cloud PDF tools are fine for routine files but never for confidential ones, even when the vendor is ISO 27001-certified and GDPR-compliant
- Distrust of 'files are deleted after processing' claims — deletion is unverifiable and privacy policies are seen as opaque, leading to 'upload-and-pray' skepticism about random PDF websites
- Compliance risk framing: users explicitly cite HIPAA-violation and privilege-waiver risk as reasons to avoid iLovePDF/Smallpdf for medical and legal documents
- Reddit users are cautious about tools.pdf24.org for sensitive files specifically because the online version requires uploads (the offline PDF24 Creator is preferred for that reason)
- The core objection is the cloud model itself — transmission and temporary server storage of files — not any specific vendor breach; in nearly every Reddit thread someone recommends offline Ghostscript/PDFtk or self-hosted Stirling PDF instead
- HN users want local tools partly to protect less-technical family members and colleagues from 'uploading sensitive documents to random PDF manipulation websites'
